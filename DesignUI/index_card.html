Scott Johnston, BSc., MBA

I started my career as a programmer / data analyst doing medical research for internationally sponsored project in South Africa. After initially getting my feet wet in the world of software development I moved to London and got involved in a public transport related project which included achievements such as building the largest wireless network in London enabling the London River Service to tell passengers arrival and departure in real time.

I then moved to Spain where I learned Spanish and worked in a data centre managing large scale systems for large multinationals such as the Spanish government, Telefonica and Santander. I then invested my savings and invested them on in stock exchange, doubling my money which enabled me to study a MBA and PGCert in Engineering from the Open University, which involved many long nights. I studied both synchronously.

Once completed my studies I moved back to London where I met a reclusive billionare based in Monaco, where I managed all his hardware and software infrastructure. I also got involved in investment activities. This period enabled me to build up my own investments through a mix of property, finance and technology startups.
